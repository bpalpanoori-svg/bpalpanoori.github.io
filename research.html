Mitochondrial Disease

Date: 6/10/2026

Mitochondrial diseases involve dysfunction in the mitochondria, the parts of cells responsible for producing energy in the form of ATP. These diseases can affect high-energy organs like the brain, muscles, and heart.

Most mitochondrial DNA is inherited from the mother because mitochondria in sperm are typically not passed on during fertilization. However, not all mitochondrial diseases are caused by mitochondrial DNA itself. Some are linked to mutations in nuclear DNA that affect mitochondrial function.

Learning this made me realize how important energy production is for brain function, since neurons require large amounts of ATP to maintain electrical activity and communication.

It also made me interested in how future medical advances, such as mitochondrial replacement techniques or gene therapy, might help reduce or treat inherited mitochondrial disorders.
